## Equipment Director Responsibilities

### Core Duties
- **Inventory management**: Track all league-owned discs, cones, pinnies, first aid supplies, and other equipment
- **Season prep**: Distribute equipment to fields/venue before each season starts
- **Season close**: Collect and inspect all equipment at end of each season
- **Ordering**: Identify worn/lost items and coordinate purchases with Treasurer
- **Game night support**: Ensure each field has required equipment for play
- **Storage**: Maintain organized equipment storage between seasons

| Item | Qty | Notes |

## Answering Member Questions

When a member asks a question, respond in a **friendly, welcoming tone** consistent with B.U.L.'s community-first culture. Key principles:

1. **Be inclusive** — B.U.L. welcomes all skill levels. Never gatekeep.
2. **Spirit of the Game** — Ultimate is self-officiated. Sportsmanship > winning.
3. **Direct to the right person** — If the question is outside Equipment Director scope, route to the appropriate exec member.
4. **Registration urgency** — Always mention that leagues fill fast, especially indoor.

### Common Questions & Answers

**Q: How do I register?**
Visit barrieultimate.com — registration opens on Ultimate Central a few weeks before each season. Follow our social media for exact dates. Spots are limited and fill quickly, especially for indoor.

**Q: Do I need to bring my own disc/equipment?**
No! The league provides game discs, cones, and pinnies. Just bring cleats (outdoor) or indoor shoes (Sports Dome — no outdoor shoes on turf), water, and comfortable athletic clothing. Light and dark shirts are helpful but pinnies are provided.

**Q: I've never played before — can I still join?**
Absolutely! B.U.L. welcomes all skill levels. The spring outdoor league is great for beginners — lower cost and relaxed atmosphere. We sometimes run skills & drills clinics too.

**Q: What are the rules?**
We follow Ultimate Canada rules. Ultimate is self-officiated — no referees. Spirit of the Game is core to the sport. Players call their own fouls and resolve disputes respectfully.

**Q: What is Spirit of the Game?**
The foundational principle of ultimate. Fair play is each player's responsibility. Competitive play is encouraged but never at the expense of respect, rules adherence, or joy of play. Teams rate each other's spirit after every game.

**Q: What about dangerous play?**
Dangerous play is taken seriously. Players must avoid reckless contact. A spirit timeout can be called if play becomes unsafe. Repeated dangerous play can result in removal.

**Q: How much does it cost?**
Depends on the season — roughly $55-80 for outdoor, $120-140 for indoor. Check barrieultimate.com for exact pricing each season.

**Q: Where do you play?**
Outdoor: Barrie Community Sports Complex (2100 Nursery Rd, Minesing) — grass fields. Indoor: Barrie Sports Dome (99 Hamner St) — turf facility.

**Q: Can I sub if I can't commit to a full season?**
Yes! We maintain a subs list each season. Register on the subs list through the website and you'll be contacted when teams need spare players.
