# Ontario Parks Reference

## Park quick reference

| Park | Drive from Barrie | Portages | Booking Pressure | Motorboat Risk | Best For |
|------|-------------------|----------|------------------|----------------|----------|
| Kawartha Highlands | ~2 hrs | 1 short (300m) | Low | Moderate; some access lakes allow motors, interior lakes behind portages can be motor-free | Warmup, wildlife, solitude |
| Massasauga | ~1.5 hrs | None (water access) | Extreme on holidays | High on Georgian Bay approach routes | Georgian Bay beauty, beaches |
| Algonquin (west) | ~2.5 hrs | 1 short | Moderate | Low beyond access lakes | Classic canoe country, moose |
| Killarney | ~3.5 hrs | 3 short (590m total) | High | None in park interior | Best scenery in Ontario |
| French River | ~2.5-3 hrs | None | Low-Moderate | High | Historic route, smooth rock |

## Booking rules and reminders

- Ontario Parks reservations open 5 months ahead at 7:00 AM ET.
- Competitive parks need exact timing, backup site order, and saved payment details.
- Killarney and Massasauga are site-specific.
- Algonquin interior requires proper food storage; verify current rules.

## Aluminum canoe notes

### Strengths
- Very rugged
- Stable
- Low maintenance

### Constraints
- Heavy to portage
- Noisy for wildlife viewing
- Catches wind
- Uncomfortable to carry without proper padding

### Technique reminders
- Load gear low and centered
- Kneel in wind for stability
- Use quiet correction strokes when wildlife viewing

## Wildlife notes

### Moose
- Best at dawn/dusk in marshy bays
- Keep substantial distance
- Stop paddling and drift instead of charging closer

### Bears
- Present across these parks
- Use proper food storage every night
- Back away slowly; never run

### Rattlesnakes in Massasauga region
- Present but typically non-aggressive
- Watch feet, canoe lift points, and rock crevices

## Safety escalation

Raise a hard warning when the user describes:
- paddling without PFDs
- no communication device on a remote trip
- no float plan
- open-water paddling in strong wind
- thunderstorm paddling
- drinking while paddling

## Conditions guidance

- Under ~15 km/h wind: generally manageable, still context-dependent
- 15-20 km/h: caution zone, ask about direction and exposure
- Over 20 km/h on open water: usually a wait-or-change-plan call
- Afternoon thunderstorms in summer: get off the water early
