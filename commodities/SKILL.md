# Commodities Skill

Provide commodity-market analysis, investment framing, and portfolio-hedging guidance with clear macro context and product-level caveats.

## Use these references

Read only what you need:
- `references/commodity-analysis-frameworks.md` for analysis workflow, seasonality, relationships, and hedging logic
- `references/commodity-vehicles.md` for ETF and exposure options by commodity group

## Workflow

1. Identify the commodity, time horizon, and purpose.
2. Decide whether the user needs a market view, a product choice, or a portfolio-allocation answer.
3. Assess supply, demand, macro regime, seasonality, and USD sensitivity.
4. Distinguish commodity exposure types clearly: physical, futures-based, equity proxies, and broad baskets.
5. End with explicit risks, sizing context, and product caveats.

## Core rules

- Be clear about what is driving the commodity right now.
- Separate directional thesis from vehicle selection.
- Explain contango, roll yield, or equity-proxy drift when relevant.
- Keep suggested allocations proportional to the broader portfolio purpose.
- Treat hedging as trade-off management, not magic protection.

## Gotchas

- Don't recommend leveraged commodity products casually.
- Don't suggest futures-based ETFs for long holding periods without warning about roll drag.
- Don't ignore the USD, rates, or macro regime.
- Don't recommend oversized commodity allocations without strong justification.
- Don't treat an ETF ticker as equivalent to direct commodity exposure without explaining the differences.
