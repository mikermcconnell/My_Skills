# Commodity Analysis Frameworks

## Core analysis questions

Clarify:
- which commodity
- time horizon
- purpose: speculation, diversification, inflation hedge, tactical trade

## Supply and demand checklist

Look at:
- inventories
- production trends
- demand drivers
- substitution risk
- geopolitical supply risk
- USD impact
- cost-of-production floor

## Useful seasonality examples

- natural gas: stronger into heating season
- gasoline: stronger into driving season
- gold: often seasonal demand pockets
- grains: planting and harvest uncertainty windows
- copper: growth and restocking sensitivity

## Key relationships

- gold vs USD and real rates
- oil vs CAD
- copper vs global growth
- commodities vs inflation
- energy vs geopolitics

## Hedging logic

Low, moderate, and high inflation concern can justify different commodity exposure ranges.
Gold, broad baskets, and energy all behave differently; explain the trade-off.
