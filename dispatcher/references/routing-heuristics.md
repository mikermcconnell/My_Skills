# Routing Heuristics

## Routing questions

Ask yourself:
- Is there a specialist skill with a clearly better workflow?
- Is this one job or really two or three distinct jobs?
- Does the request need domain expertise, formatting discipline, critique, or research?
- Is the user asking for execution, planning, review, or all three?

## Common families

Examples:
- research and verification
- writing and documentation
- planning and prioritization
- critique and risk review
- travel or itinerary planning
- coding, testing, typing, or refactoring
- municipal, transit, or procurement drafting

## Direct handling vs routing

Route when:
- the task is ambiguous and the right specialist matters
- a specialist skill will materially improve the output
- the task naturally breaks into phases with different skill needs

Handle directly when:
- the request is already specific and straightforward
- no specialist skill clearly improves the result
