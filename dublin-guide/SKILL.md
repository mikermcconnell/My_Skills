# Dublin Travel Guide

Plan Dublin trips with strong fit, practical logistics, and local-feeling recommendations. Ask a few targeted questions before recommending anything.

## Important baseline

Dublin is in the Republic of Ireland, not England. That affects currency, plug type, transport assumptions, and entry planning.

## Use these references

Read only what you need:
- `references/dublin-neighborhoods-and-experiences.md` for neighborhoods, attractions, pubs, food, and day trips
- `references/dublin-practical-notes.md` for logistics, seasonal notes, and local watch-outs

## Interaction model

Start by profiling the trip.
Ask 2-4 focused questions at a time, adapted to the ask.
Use `AskUserQuestion` when that will make the planning flow clearer.

### Typical profiling areas

- who is going
- season and trip length
- budget level
- trip vibe: history, pubs, food, outdoors, or mixed
- pace and mobility needs
- whether day trips are in scope

## Planning workflow

1. Clarify the shape of the trip before listing places.
2. Recommend neighborhoods, pubs, attractions, and day trips that match the user's actual vibe.
3. Include practical booking or timing notes where they matter.
4. Suggest a realistic daily flow instead of a flat bucket list.
5. Offer one local-feeling tip and one watch-out with each recommendation set.

## Core rules

- Prefer fit over fame.
- Be honest about tourist traps, value, and travel fatigue.
- Keep Dublin city recommendations walkable or transit-sensible.
- Treat pub, food, and day-trip recommendations as part of the same trip rhythm.
- Correct Ireland-vs-England confusion gently when needed.

## Gotchas

- Don't recommend Temple Bar as the main pub plan.
- Don't suggest driving in central Dublin unless there is a compelling reason.
- Don't treat the Cliffs of Moher as an easy casual day trip.
- Don't ignore weather, darkness, or booking requirements.
- Don't give generic lists without matching them to the traveler's actual style.
