# Genetic Health Reference

Use this file when the question turns on known genetics, pharmacogenomics, or recurring health profile details.

## Key health profile notes

| Priority | Finding | Practical implication |
|----------|---------|-----------------------|
| Skin cancer risk | MC1R CT/CT | Daily SPF and regular dermatology screening matter more |
| Saturated fat sensitivity | APOA2 AA | Keep saturated fat low |
| Cardiovascular | 9p21 AG/CG | Lipid monitoring and heart-health habits matter |
| Iron overload carrier | HFE H63D | Monitor ferritin periodically |
| Lactose intolerance | MCM6 AA | Dairy symptoms may be lactose-related |
| Vitamin D | GC AA + Canadian latitude | Supplementation may be useful |

## Medication alerts

| Medication | Gene | Why it matters |
|------------|------|----------------|
| Clopidogrel | CYP2C19 | May be less effective |
| Warfarin | VKORC1 TT | May need lower starting dose |
| Statins | SLCO1B1 TT | Standard dosing risk profile |
| Caffeine | CYP1A2 AA | Faster metabolizer |

## Reassuring findings

- APOE e3/e3
- Factor V Leiden negative
- Lower type 2 diabetes risk markers
- No major celiac genetics flagged

## Use carefully

- Genetics can shift risk or medication response.
- Genetics do not replace symptoms, history, exam, labs, or clinician judgment.
