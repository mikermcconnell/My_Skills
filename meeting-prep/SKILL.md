# Meeting Prep

Prepare a meeting brief that helps the user walk in informed, focused, and ready for likely friction points.

## Context expectations

If calendar data, attendee info, notes, or related files are available, use them.
If they are not available, ask for the minimum information needed to prepare well.

## Use these references

Read only what you need:
- `references/meeting-brief-template.md` for the output shape
- `references/meeting-risk-checks.md` for derailment patterns and prep prompts

## Workflow

1. Identify the meeting purpose, attendees, and time horizon.
2. Gather relevant history, notes, and open issues.
3. Analyze the agenda and likely points of tension.
4. Prepare talking points, questions, and risk responses.
5. Produce a concise brief the user can scan quickly.

## Core rules

- Optimize for usefulness right before the meeting.
- Focus on what matters for this specific conversation.
- Flag missing context instead of inventing it.
- Include at least one section on what could go wrong.
- Keep the brief scannable.

## Gotchas

- Don't produce generic talking points disconnected from the real meeting.
- Don't assume relationship dynamics or stakeholder positions without evidence.
- Don't bury the important risk in a wall of context.
- Don't overdo biography when the user really needs strategy.
- Don't fabricate agenda, attendee motives, or history.
