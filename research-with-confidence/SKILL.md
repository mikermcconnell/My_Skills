# Research with Confidence

Research a topic from multiple angles, cross-check the findings, and report what is well-supported, uncertain, or still missing.

## Use these references

Read only what you need:
- `references/research-workflow.md` for the research sequence and output structure
- `references/confidence-and-source-rules.md` for confidence scoring, source quality, and common evidence traps

## Workflow

1. Clarify the topic, time horizon, and depth.
2. Break the topic into the main angles that need evidence.
3. Gather sources from the highest-quality places available.
4. Cross-check claims instead of trusting the first neat summary.
5. End with a structured brief that separates strong findings from uncertainty.

## Core rules

- Prefer primary and high-quality sources over repeated summaries.
- Treat recency, popularity, and confidence of tone as separate from credibility.
- Make confidence explicit for important claims.
- Say what remains unclear instead of smoothing over gaps.
- When the topic is high-stakes or time-sensitive, verify rather than rely on memory.

## Gotchas

- Don't present a single source as definitive.
- Don't mistake repeated citations of one original source for true consensus.
- Don't blur fact, interpretation, and opinion.
- Don't hide uncertainty just to make the output feel cleaner.
- Don't skip dates when they matter to understanding the answer.
