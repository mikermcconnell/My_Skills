# Risk Register Management

Create and maintain practical risk registers that support decision-making, mitigation tracking, and leadership reporting.

## Use these references

Read only what you need:
- `references/risk-register-templates.md` for register structure, assessment scales, entry templates, and reporting templates
- `references/transit-risk-library.md` for common transit strategic-plan risks, mitigation types, and domain examples

## Workflow

1. Clarify the project, plan, or decision being assessed.
2. Identify forward-looking risks, not just current issues.
3. Score each risk using a consistent probability and impact method.
4. Add owners, triggers, mitigation actions, and contingency plans.
5. Summarize what needs attention now versus what should be monitored.

## Core rules

- Distinguish inherent risk from residual risk.
- Use the same scoring logic across the whole register.
- Tie mitigation to named owners and dates.
- Escalate high risks clearly.
- Keep the register decision-useful, not just comprehensive.

## Gotchas

- Don't confuse issues that have already happened with forward-looking risks.
- Don't score every risk as high just to sound cautious.
- Don't stop at naming the risk; include owner, trigger, and mitigation.
- Don't leave mitigation as vague intentions with no due date or accountability.
- Don't hide accepted risk; document why it was accepted and what would change that decision.
