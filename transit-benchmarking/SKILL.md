# Transit Agency Benchmarking

Conduct systematic peer agency comparisons using standardized metrics and structured analysis.

## Peer Selection Criteria

Select 6-10 peer agencies based on:

| Factor | Weight | Considerations |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Population size | High | Within 50-200% of target agency |
| Growth rate | High | Similar trajectory (stable, moderate, high growth) |
| Service type | Medium | Conventional, on-demand mix similar |
| Governance | Medium | Municipal vs. regional authority |
| Geography | Medium | Similar climate, urban form |
| Provincial context | Low | Ontario peers for regulatory alignment |

## Standard Metrics Framework

### Service Metrics
| Metric | Formula | Good Range |
|--------|---------|------------|
| Revenue hours/capita | Annual revenue hours ÷ population | 0.8-1.5 |
| Rides/capita | Annual ridership ÷ population | 20-50 |
| Rides/revenue hour | Annual ridership ÷ revenue hours | 20-35 |
| Service coverage | % population within 400m of stop | 80-95% |
| Peak frequency | Minutes between buses on busiest routes | 10-20 min |

### Financial Metrics
| Metric | Formula | Good Range |
|--------|---------|------------|
| Cost/revenue hour | Operating cost ÷ revenue hours | $100-150 |
| Cost/ride | Operating cost ÷ ridership | $4-8 |
| Farebox recovery | Fare revenue ÷ operating cost | 20-40% |
| Subsidy/ride | (Cost - revenue) ÷ ridership | $3-6 |
| Cost/capita | Operating cost ÷ population | $80-150 |

### Fleet Metrics
| Metric | Formula | Good Range |
|--------|---------|------------|
| Fleet age (average) | Sum of vehicle ages ÷ fleet size | 6-10 years |
| Spare ratio | (Total - peak) ÷ peak × 100 | 15-20% |
| Buses/1000 capita | Fleet size ÷ (population/1000) | 0.2-0.4 |
| Zero-emission % | ZEB count ÷ total fleet × 100 | Varies |

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Define scope** - What question is benchmarking answering?
2. **Select peers** - 6-10 agencies using criteria above
3. **Gather data** - Use template, note data year/source
4. **Normalize metrics** - Per capita, per hour standardization
5. **Build comparison matrix** - Visual side-by-side
6. **Identify outliers** - Investigate high/low performers
7. **Document best practices** - What can Barrie learn?
8. **Draft recommendations** - Actionable next steps

## Gotchas

- Comparing raw numbers without per-capita normalization
- Ignoring regional context (GO Transit, university presence)
- Using stale data (pre-pandemic vs. post-pandemic)
- Cherry-picking metrics that favor predetermined conclusions
- Ignoring service type differences (conventional vs. on-demand ratios)
